Enter the absorbance readings, separated by commas.

It is calculated using the following equation:

\[\frac{\triangle A}{\triangle t}\]

where \(\Delta A \) represents the absorbance difference after a particular time, and \(\Delta t \) represents the corresponding time difference.

In this calculation, the initial velocity is determined over a time interval of 60 seconds. It is assumed that absorbance readings are taken every 30 seconds, starting from the initial time of 0 seconds.

Similarly, we calculate the initial velocity for different substrate concentrations to determine the Km and Vmax values.
